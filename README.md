# Incident Report: Healthcare Ransomware Attack
> **Security incident analysis completed during the Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate.**
> *Focus: Incident documentation, attack vector identification, and the 5 W's framework.*

---

## 1. Scenario Overview
A primary-care health clinic experienced a severe security breach on a Tuesday morning at 9:00 a.m. Business operations were completely halted as employees lost access to critical medical records and patient data due to file encryption.

---

## 2. Incident Analysis (The 5 W's)

Based on my investigation of the event, here are the key details documented in the incident handler's journal:

* **Who:** An organized group of unethical hackers known for targeting the healthcare and transportation sectors.
* **What:** A targeted ransomware attack that encrypted internal files and displayed a ransom note demanding payment for a decryption key.
* **When:** Tuesday morning, at approximately 9:00 a.m.
* **Where:** A small U.S.-based healthcare clinic.
* **Why:** The attackers gained access through **targeted phishing emails** sent to employees. A malicious attachment was downloaded, allowing the deployment of malware and subsequent ransomware to extort money from the organization.

---

## 3. Attack Vector & Impact
The investigation reveals a classic multi-stage attack:
1.  **Initial Access:** Social engineering via phishing.
2.  **Execution:** Employee interaction with a malicious attachment.
3.  **Impact:** Encryption of mission-critical software and patient files, leading to a total operational shutdown.

---

## 4. Post-Incident Reflections
To improve the organization's security posture, the following questions must be addressed:
* **Prevention:** How can we implement better email filtering and employee security awareness training to spot phishing? 
* **Recovery:** Does the organization have offline backups to restore data without paying the ransom? 
* **Policy:** What is the legal and ethical stance on paying ransoms to criminal groups? 

---

## 5. Summary
This project demonstrates my ability to objectively document a security crisis using an incident handler's journal. By breaking down the attack into the 5 W's, I can provide clear, actionable information to stakeholders and technical teams for future remediation.
